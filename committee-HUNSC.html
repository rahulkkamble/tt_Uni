HUNSC

Historical United Nations Security Council

Agenda

Deliberating upon the fall of the North Tower of the World Trade Centre, emphasizing on the threat of 9/11 attacks and reassessing the role of terrorist groups and belligerents in regards to the attack. (Freeze Date: 8 55 am GMT-5 , 11th September 2001)

Description

The HUNSC(Historical United Nations Security Council) is an advanced and specialized historical crisis committee wherein delegates step into the shoes of UNSC members and address past global crises. The catch is, that the delegates are sent back to a freeze date as of which the conflict is happening. What HUNSC does different from every other committee is that it focuses on rewriting history through rigorous debate, allowing delegates to dive deep into challenging issues that have had a huge impact on current geopolitics and the rest of the world. This is what makes it the best committee out there! Throughout the conference, delegates will face a building crisis, foundationing from reality, and will draft resolutions to solve it.

Delegates participating will build adaptability, thinking skills to think creatively and a new perspective on world affairs. They will be given the power and responsibility to shape the world and for the first time ever, fix the past! We wish to witness the most unique ideas and most unexpected alliances in the face of every pressing crisis update. And remember, the best friend a delegate can have in a historical committee is research. Any action or fact occurring after the given freeze date can land you in trouble! However, we can confidently say that HUNSC is the most exciting committee, characterized by a mixture of UNSC and crisis!

Vedant Bahri

Co-Secretary General
