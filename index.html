Letter from the
Co-Secretary General

Dear Delegates,

I am Kevin Trivedi, your Co-Secretary-General for the first edition of JBCN Chembur MSMUN. It is my honour to welcome you to this conference. I do this with a great sense of responsibility and pride.

Having started my MUNing journey years ago, when I was in grade 4, I have attended numerous conferences, where I have learned leadership, discipline, and perseverance. My approach to MUN has been influenced by the discipline born both within and outside committee rooms. As a middle-distance runner who competes in demanding events such as the 400 and 800 metres, I have come to realize that success is achieved through sustained effort, execution, and resilience, and not by momentary bursts of motivation. These learnings translate directly into MUN, where lengthy sessions, evolving debates, and uncertainty require great composure and tenacity.

Besides MUN, I have taken part in numerous competitions, including the World Scholar's Cup at Yale University, in which I received several medals during participation. These experiences led me to a guiding principle: excellence is built through preparation, adaptability, and discipline.

My love for history leads me to draw inspiration from the Roman Empire, whose strength did not necessarily lie in conquest but in structure, law, and a steadfast sense of duty. Roman policy placed order, responsibility, and service to the state at the forefront, principles that still play a pivotal role in effective leadership and diplomacy today. In MUN, authority is established not through volume but through consistency and adherence to procedure.

Within the committee, the best delegates debate with clarity, not with noise. MUN pushes delegates to argue a position and negotiate, and understand accountability. You will be put into a position where you have to represent views perhaps contrary to your own, make decisions when the pressure mounts, and juggle many activities at once, experiences that will serve you well in life, well beyond the conference.

To all delegates, regardless of whether it is your first mun or the hundredth, I urge you to participate wholeheartedly, seriously, and rigorously. Measure success not solely by result but by the learnings you take away.

I look forward to a conference defined by rigorous debate, structure, and innovative paperwork.

“The price of greatness is responsibility.” — Winston Churchill

Kevin Trivedi

Co-Secretary General

Vedant Bahri

Co-Secretary General

Letter from the
Co-Secretary General

Dear Delegates,

Greetings to the future of this world, my dear delegates! My name is Vedant Bahri and I am honoured to serve you as your co-secretary general for the JBCN Chembur Middle School MUN 2026.

I started my MUNning journey a few years ago and honestly fell in love with MUNning in no time. I believe that MUN conferences are the best and most impactful platforms to build public speaking skills, voice your opinions and develop perspectives of pressing global issues. Not just that, I believe that a MUN allows you to build a social network and allows you to build diplomatic relations with random strangers who become best friends(or enemies) after just a two-day conference!

MUN has taught me that it is not important to have the loudest voice in the room, the most important is to have the most effective ideas and convince people that you are the only logical delegate in that very room. My MUNning journey has allowed me to put myself in the shoes of a head of state of a nation and formulate my own distinctive strategies and dossiers. From googling “What is a draft resolution” to successfully drafting and passing many, MUNs have given me confidence and the guts to voice out my opinions.

Beyond MUNs, I’m an avid non-fiction reader and love reading about business, politics and current affairs. I lead a Robotics Club at my school, JBCN school Chembur. Moreover, I love playing football, defending Christiano Ronaldo’s controversial statements after his interviews and watching Rohit Sharma completely obliterate the opposition!

If you’re a first timer in MUN, or a seasoned MUNner, remember that the sole purpose of a MUN is not to win an award; it’s an area where you challenge yourself, think like global leaders and put yourself out there in the most difficult situations. Whether it’s UNO reversing a crisis that has the potential to break you, or having the most inexperienced delegates in your bloc, only the most adaptable delegate survives! In a MUN, it’s completely normal to defend a country you never knew existed before the MUN! Don’t fear public speaking! My advice to first timers is to be well-researched, confident and participate avidly and passionately in committee! As Frederick Blesse rightly said, “No guts No glory”. To win, you have to endure, sustain and lead the committee, adapting to your nation's foreign policy best!

To all delegates, the need for change comes from within. My advice to all delegates is to utilize this platform and get the best out of this two-day conference. Step out of your comfort zone, and lead the herd! I’m extremely exhilarated to meet each and every one of you, understanding our futuremakers and having the best MUN conference! Our MUN team faithfully and sincerely works to make this MUN delightful for each one of you.

Remember, “With great power, comes great responsibility”, so we expect each one of you to live up to this responsibility. Lastly and most importantly, enjoy the conference.

I wish each one of you’ll best of luck and hope you won’t need it!
